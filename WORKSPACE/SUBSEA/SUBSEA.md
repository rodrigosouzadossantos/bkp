# Subsea Concepts


## Subsea Goals
- **Subsea** is a concept that allows for the creation of multiple, isolated
  environments within a centralized view in order to concentrate related
  projects, resources. It works like a namespace or a logical grouping of
  subjects, providing a way to organize and manage resources, policies, and
  configurations in a more efficient and scalable manner.
  - Subsea allows for better resource management, as it enables teams to
    allocate resources based on specific needs and requirements, rather than
    having a single, monolithic environment. This can lead to improved
    performance, reduced costs, and better overall management of resources.
  - Subsea also promotes better collaboration and communication among teams, as
    it provides a clear structure for organizing projects and resources. This
    can lead to improved productivity and efficiency, as teams can easily find
    and access the resources they need to complete their work.
  - Think it as an umbrella or container that encompasses multiple projects,
    resources, and policies, allowing for better organization and management of
    resources across different teams and projects. Each protect can have its own
    subset of resources, policies, and configurations, enabling teams to work
    independently while sharing the underlying infrastructure.


## Projects
- **Projects** are individual units of work that are organized within Subsea
  umbrella. They represent specific initiatives, tasks, or objectives that teams
  are working on. Projects can be associated with specific resources, policies,
  and configurations, allowing for better management and organization of work.
  - Projects can be used to track progress, manage resources, and collaborate
    with team members. They can also be used to set goals and objectives, and to
    measure the success of initiatives.
  - Projects can be organized in a hierarchical structure, with parent and child
    projects, allowing for better organization and management of work. This can
    help teams to break down larger initiatives into smaller, more manageable
    tasks, and to track progress more effectively.
  - Projects can also be associated with specific policies and configurations,
    allowing for better management of resources and ensuring that teams are
    following best practices and guidelines. This can help to improve the
    overall quality of work and to ensure that teams are working efficiently and
    effectively.


## Resources
- **Resources** are the various assets, tools, and services that teams use to
  complete their work. They can include things like computing resources,
  storage, software tools, and other assets that are necessary for completing
  projects and initiatives. Resources can be associated with specific projects,
  allowing for better management and organization of work.
  - Resources can be allocated based on specific needs and requirements,
    allowing for better performance and reduced costs. This can help teams to
    optimize their use of resources and to ensure that they are using resources
    efficiently and effectively.
  - Resources can also be shared across different projects, allowing for better
    collaboration and communication among teams. This can help to improve
    productivity and efficiency, as teams can easily access the resources they
    need to complete their work.
  - Resources can also be associated with specific policies and configurations,
    allowing for better management of resources and ensuring that teams are
    following best practices and guidelines. This can help to improve the overall
    quality of work and to ensure that teams are working efficiently and
    effectively.


## Policies
- **Policies** are the rules, guidelines, and best practices that govern how
  teams manage and use resources within project. They can include things like
  security policies, access control policies, resource allocation policies, and
  other guidelines that help to ensure that teams are working efficiently and
  effectively.
  - Policies can be used to ensure that teams are following best practices and
    guidelines, which can help to improve the overall quality of work and to
    ensure that teams are working efficiently and effectively. This can help to
    reduce errors, improve security, and ensure that teams are using resources
    in a responsible and effective manner.
  - Policies can also be used to manage access to resources, ensuring that only
    authorized users have access to sensitive information and resources. This
    can help to improve security and to protect sensitive information from
    unauthorized access.
  - Policies can also be used to manage resource allocation, ensuring that teams
    are using resources efficiently and effectively. This can help to reduce
    costs and to optimize the use of resources across different projects and
    initiatives.

## Code
- **Code** refers to the actual programming code that teams write to implement
  their projects and initiatives. It can include things like source code,
  scripts, and other types of code that are necessary for completing projects
  and initiatives. Code can be associated with specific projects, allowing for
  better management and organization of work.
  - Code can be managed using version control systems, allowing teams to track
    changes, collaborate with team members, and manage different versions of
    their code. This can help to improve collaboration and to ensure that teams
    are working efficiently and effectively.
  - Code can also be associated with specific policies and configurations,
    allowing for better management of resources and ensuring that teams are
    following best practices and guidelines. This can help to improve the
    overall quality of work and to ensure that teams are working efficiently and
    effectively.
  - Code can also be used to automate tasks and processes, allowing teams to
    improve efficiency and to reduce manual work. This can help to improve
    productivity and to ensure that teams are working efficiently and
    effectively.

  Common code can be shared across different projects, allowing for better
collaboration and communication among teams. This can help to improve
productivity and efficiency, as teams can easily access the code they need to
complete their work.

  By sharing common code, teams can also reduce duplication of effort and ensure
that they are using consistent and standardized code across different projects
and initiatives. This can help to improve the overall quality of work and to
ensure that teams are working efficiently and effectively.

  To facilitate the sharing of common code, the Subsea will be itself a namespace
that can contain common code that can be accessed and used by different
projects. This allows teams to easily share code and to ensure that they are
using consistent and standardized code across different projects and
initiatives.

  By using the Subsea as a namespace for common code, teams can improve
collaboration and communication among team members, and can ensure that they are
working efficiently and effectively, while also reducing duplication of effort
and improving the overall quality of work.
